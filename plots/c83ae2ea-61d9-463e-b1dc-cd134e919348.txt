1. Opening Image: Romanus is a young boy living in poverty in Germany. He's orphaned at a young age and has to fend for himself. Despite his difficult circumstances, Romanus is a survivor. He's street-smart and has a sense of humor.

2. Theme Stated: Romanus meets a wealthy man named Faustus who takes an interest in him. Faustus offers to help Romanus improve his life and makes him an offer he can't refuse. Romanus begins working for Faustus and quickly rises through the ranks. However, Faustus's true intentions become clear and Romanus must decide whether to betray the only person who has ever cared for him or to stay loyal and risk everything.

3. Setup: Romanus is struggling to survive on the streets of Germany. He's constantly cold, hungry, and tired. He does whatever it takes to get by, even if it means stealing or begging.

4. Catalyst: Romanus meets Faustus, who offers to help him improve his life. Romanus begins working for Faustus and quickly learns how to survive and thrive in Germany's cutthroat world.

5. Debate: Romanus starts to see Faustus for the manipulative opportunist he really is. Faustus starts making increasingly dangerous demands of Romanus, who knows that if he doesn't comply, he'll be killed. Romanus is caught between his loyalty to Faustus and his own self-preservation.

6. Break Into Two: Romanus falls in love with a woman named Helene. He's determined to find a way to be with her, even though she's from a wealthy family and completely out of his league.

7. B Story: Romanus's relationship with Helene provides a counterpoint to the increasingly dark and dangerous world he's caught up in with Faustus. While Faustus is manipulating and blackmailing Romanus, Helene represents hope and love.

8. Fun and Games: Romanus and Helene's relationship blossoms and they're happy together. However, Faustus finds out about their relationship and uses it to his advantage. He blackmails Romanus into doing an even more dangerous job for him.

9. Midpoint: Romanus is faced with an impossible choice. If he doesn't comply with Faustus's demands, Faustus will kill Helene. Romanus makes a deal with the Devil, not knowing what the consequences will be. He sells his soul to Faustus in exchange for Helene's safety.

10. Bad Guys Close In: Faustus is a master of deception and he doesn't hold up his end of the bargain. Romanus loses everything: his wealth, his position, and his liberty. Imprisoned and tortured, Romanus suffers for his foolishness.

11. All is Lost: Romanus is at the lowest point in his life. He's lost everything and he's been betrayed by the only person who ever cared for him. He's given up all hope of ever escaping from Faustus's clutches.

12. Dark Night of the Soul: Romanus is in prison, tortured and alone. He regrets ever making the deal with Faustus. He regrets ever meeting the man who has ruined his life.

13. Break Into Three: Just when Romanus has given up all hope, Helene comes to see him in prison. She's been searching for him ever since he disappeared. She tells him that she still loves him and she's going to help him escape.

14. Finale: Romanus and Helene make their escape and start a new life together. They're finally free of Faustus's control. They're happy and in love, and they have each other.

15. Final Image: Romanus and Helene are living happily together, free from the clutches of Faustus. They're in love and they have each other.
