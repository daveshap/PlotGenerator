1. Opening Image: The story opens with a description of the world before recorded history. This world is ruled by powerful magical creatures and humans are little more than their slaves.

2. Theme Stated: The theme of the story is freedom. Alaria, the main character, dreams of one day being free from the yoke of her masters.

3. Setup: The story introduces Alaria, a young woman who is enslaved by the magical creatures.

4. Catalyst: Alaria is out swimming in the sea when she is attacked by a large and ferocious sea monster.

5. Debate: Alaria fights back with all her might and eventually kills the creature. From its carcass, she retrieves a magical stone that allows her to breathe underwater.

6. Break Into Two: Alaria begins to explore the underwater world and quickly discovers that she is not the only one with magic. She meets other humans with similar abilities and together they form a secret society dedicated to freeing themselves and all humans from the tyranny of their masters.

7. B Story: The story of the rebel group's fight against their oppressors is interwoven with the story of Alaria's personal journey.

8. Fun and Games: For years, Alaria and her fellow rebels fight against their oppressors, using their powers to strike back at those who sought to enslave them. They have many successes, but also suffer many defeats.

9. Midpoint: Alaria becomes one of the most powerful members of the rebel group. She is fiercely dedicated to the cause and would do whatever it took to see freedom for all humans.

10. Bad Guys Close In: The rebels receive a tip that a group of humans are being transported to a nearby island where they would be sold into slavery. Alaria and a team of rebels go to rescue them, but they are ambushed by the slavers.

11. All is Lost: In the ensuing battle, Alaria is gravely wounded. The rebels are able to defeat the slavers and rescue the humans, but at the cost of Alaria's life.

12. Dark Night of the Soul: Alaria's death is a blow to the rebel group, but her sacrifice spurs them on to continue the fight for freedom. In her honor, they begin teaching others the ways of magic so that one day all humans might be free.

13. Break Into Three: The story concludes with the legend of Alaria, the first rebel and freedom fighter. Many years later, her story is still told to those who wish to learn the ways of magic. And her memory continues to inspire those who fight for freedom.

14. Finale: The story ends with the rebel group continuing to fight for freedom in Alaria's memory.

15. Final Image: The final image is of Alaria's grave, which is marked by a stone with the magical symbol of freedom.
