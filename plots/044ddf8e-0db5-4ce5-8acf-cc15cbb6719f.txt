1. Opening Image: The village is a small, isolated community in the North Atlantic. The villagers are poor and struggling to survive.

2. Theme Stated: The stranger arrives in the village and tells the villagers that they are living in an alternate timeline. He says that in the original timeline, the village was a prosperous fishing community.

3. Setup: The stranger starts to tell the villagers about all the events that have led to their current circumstances. The villager's listen in rapt attention as the stranger tells them about the series of natural disasters that befell their village in the original timeline.

4. Catalyst: The first disaster that the stranger tells the villagers about is a severe storm that damaged the village's boats and destroyed the fish stock.

5. Debate: The villagers are skeptical of the stranger's story at first, but as they start to realize that he is telling the truth, they begin to wonder if there is anything they can do to change their current circumstances.

6. Break Into Two: The villagers decide to send a delegation to the parallel universe to try and persuade the historian to help them change their timeline.

7. B Story: The delegation is successful, and the historian agrees to help the villagers. He takes them back to the moment just before the first disaster struck and shows them what they need to do to prevent it from happening.

8. Fun and Games: The villagers follow the historian's instructions and are able to avert the first disaster. This gives them the confidence to face the second disaster, and they are able to prevent that from happening as well.

9. Midpoint: With each disaster that they avert, the villagers become more and more hopeful that they can change their timeline and create a better future for themselves.

10. Bad Guys Close In: The villagers are finally able to create a prosperous future for themselves. They have a thriving fishing community, and their village is a thriving hub of trade.

11. All is Lost: The villagers are happy and content, and they owe it all to the historian who showed them the way.
