1. Opening Image: We see Armand Beauchamp arriving in France. He is a world-renowned detective, and he has been called to France to solve a mystery.

2. Theme Stated: The theme of the story is stated by Armand Beauchamp. He says that the people of France are very down to earth and have a strong sense of community. They also value their privacy and are very suspicious of outsiders.

3. Setup: We are introduced to the victim, Elise Dubois. She is a young woman who has been found murdered in her home. The big question is who did it?

4. Catalyst: Armand Beauchamp begins his investigation into the murder of Elise Dubois.

5. Debate: Armand Beauchamp quickly realizes that this is a very complicated case. There are a lot of people who had motive to kill her, and any one of them could be the killer.

6. Break Into Two: Armand Beauchamp decides to focus his investigation on the people who were closest to Elise.

7. B Story: We are introduced to the B story, which is the story of Armand's investigation.

8. Fun and Games: Armand Beauchamp begins to uncover clues that lead him to believe that Elise Dubois was killed by someone she knew.

9. Midpoint: Armand Beauchamp is able to identify the killer as Elise's husband, Pierre.

10. Bad Guys Close In: Pierre becomes more desperate and decides to start killing other people who were close to Elise.

11. All is Lost: Armand is able to put all the pieces together and identify the killer.

12. Dark Night of the Soul: Armand is able to bring Pierre to justice and bring some closure to the case. Elise's family is also able to find some peace knowing that her killer has been caught.

13. Break Into Three: The story ends with Armand Beauchamp leaving France.

14. Finale: Armand Beauchamp is able to bring the killer to justice and bring some closure to the case.

15. Final Image: Armand Beauchamp is seen leaving France.
