1. Opening Image: The kingdom of Kush is introduced. It is a land that is dry, dusty, and hot. The people are poor and life is difficult. Despite the difficulties, the people of Kush have a sense of pride in their country and their culture.

2. Theme Stated: The theme of the story is introduced. The kingdom of Kush is constantly at war with its neighbors. Many are killed, and even more are left homeless and hungry. In the midst of all this suffering, there is one man who seems to have it all. His name is Kanda, and he is the king of Kush.

3. Setup: Kanda is introduced as a ruthless ruler. He has a strong army, and he is not afraid to use it. Kanda is also a very ambitious man. He wants to expand his kingdom and make it the most powerful in all of Africa.

4. Catalyst: Kanda decides to launch a major offensive against one of his neighbors, the kingdom of Assyria.

5. Debate: Kanda's army is successful in battle, and they quickly conquer the kingdom of Assyria. The people of Kush are overjoyed, and they hail Kanda as a hero. Even though Kush is now the most powerful kingdom in Africa, Kanda is not content. He wants more.

6. Break Into Two: Kanda sets his sights on the next kingdom, Egypt. The Egyptians are a powerful people, with a strong army and a rich culture. Kanda knows that conquering them will not be easy, but he is determined to try.

7. B Story: While Kanda is preparing for war with Egypt, his wife and children are introduced. They are happy to see him and they forgive him for his past mistakes.

8. Fun and Games: Kanda's army is no match for the Egyptians, and they are quickly defeated. Kanda is captured and taken prisoner. He is brought before the Pharaoh, who is amazed by his audacity.

9. Midpoint: The Pharaoh sentences Kanda to death, but he is spared at the last minute by the queen of Egypt. She takes pity on him and decides to spare his life.

10. Bad Guys Close In: Kanda is sent back to Kush in disgrace. He is a changed man, and he is no longer the ruthless ruler he once was. Kanda's return to Kush is not welcomed by all. Some of the people see him as a failure, and they want nothing to do with him.

11. All is Lost: Kanda is a different man now, and he is no longer interested in conquest. He only wants peace for his kingdom. He knows that this can only be achieved if the people of Kush are united.

12. Dark Night of the Soul: Kanda makes a decision that will change the course of history. He asks the queen of Egypt for her hand in marriage.

13. Break Into Three: The queen accepts, and the two kingdoms are united. Kanda and the queen rule Kush together, and they bring peace and prosperity to the land.

14. Finale: The people of Kush are finally able to live in peace and harmony.

15. Final Image: The kingdom of Kush is shown to be a prosperous and peaceful land.
