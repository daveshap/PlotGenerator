1. Opening Image: The story begins with a description of the American landscape during the Stone Age. The reader is introduced to the various tribes that populate the land, and the tension that exists between them.

2. Theme Stated: The theme of the story is introduced, which is the dangers of violence.

3. Setup: The reader is introduced to the main character, John, and his tribe, the Good Samaritans. John's sister is kidnapped by a rival tribe, and he sets out to rescue her.

4. Catalyst: John quickly realizes that he is woefully unprepared for the task at hand, and resorts to seek help from a veteran warrior of the tribe, known as Bo.

5. Debate: Bo is reluctant to help John, but after much persistence, he relents. The two set out on a dangerous journey to rescue John's sister.

6. Break Into Two: John and Bo begin their journey to rescue John's sister.

7. B Story: Along the way, they encounter various challenges and obstacles, both from the natural world and from the other Stone Age tribes. They also meets others who joins their quest, including a young woman named Sarah.

8. Fun and Games: John and his companions face various challenges as they make their way to the rival tribe's village.

9. Midpoint: They reach the village and find that it is heavily fortified. They hatch a plan to sneak in under the cover of night.

10. Bad Guys Close In: Their plan quickly goes awry, and they find themselves in the midst of a battle.

11. All is Lost: In the midst of the fighting, John's sister is killed. Enraged, John lashes out and kills her killers.

12. Dark Night of the Soul: John is haunted by the violence he has witnessed and committed. These events will stay with him for the rest of his life.

13. Break Into Three: John and his companions make their way back to John's village.

14. Finale: They are all changed by the experience. John, in particular, is haunted by the violence he has witnessed and committed.

15. Final Image: The story ends with a description of the American landscape during the Stone Age. The reader is left with a sense of the violence and destruction that has taken place.
