DETAILED FINAL OUTLINE. EACH BEAT SHOULD BE A PARAGRAPH LONG:

==========

1. Opening Image: The opening image should introduce the reader to the world of the story. It should be a detailed description of the setting and the characters.

2. Theme Stated: The theme of the story should be stated in the form of a question. The question should be something that the reader can relate to and it should be something that the story will explore.

3. Setup: The setup should introduce the reader to the conflict of the story. It should be clear what the stakes are and what the characters are fighting for.

4. Catalyst: The catalyst should be the event that sets the story in motion. It should be the event that makes the characters realize that they need to take action.

5. Debate: The debate should be the scene where the characters discuss what they should do. They should debate the pros and cons of their plan and try to come to a decision.

6. Break Into Two: The break into two should be the point where the characters take action. They should decide to go through with their plan and they should start to execute it.

7. B Story: The B story should be a subplot that runs parallel to the main plot. It should be something that the characters are dealing with in addition to the main conflict.

8. Fun and Games: The fun and games should be the scenes where the characters are working towards their goal. They should be making progress and things should be going well for them.

9. Midpoint: The midpoint should be the point where the characters reach their goal. They should achieve what they set out to do and they should be successful.

10. Bad Guys Close In: The bad guys should start to close in on the characters. They should be getting closer and closer to catching them.

11. All is Lost: The all is lost moment should be the point where it seems like the characters are going to fail. They should be at their lowest point and it should seem like there is no way for them to win.

12. Dark Night of the Soul: The dark night of the soul should be the point where the characters have a moment of doubt. They should question whether or not they can win and they should start to lose hope.

13. Break Into Three: The break into three should be the point where the characters find a new resolve. They should decide to fight back and they should start to make a comeback.

14. Finale: The finale should be the climax of the story. It should be the big showdown between the characters and the bad guys.

15. Final Image: The final image should be a scene of the characters triumphantly standing over the bad guys. They should be victorious and they should be happy.
